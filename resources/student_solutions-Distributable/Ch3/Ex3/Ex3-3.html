The simplest way to convert your birthday in the current year is to use one of several calculators that are available on the web. However the conversions provided by these do not always agree. The authors of Calendrical Calculations have a conversion applet at http://emr.cs.iit.edu/home/reingold/calendar-book/second-edition/ which should provide correct results.

Another way is to do the conversion by hand as follows. Day 1 of the FR year is the most recent autumnal equinox. This is around September 23, but the date varies. The date of the equinox for a given year can be found using the algorithm found at http://image.gsfc.nasa.gov/poetry/ask/astro/q1467.html. However, this algorithm does not indicate the location for which it is calculating the equinox. You can, of course, simply assume that the equinox is on the 23rd. If your birthday is before the equinox in the current year, then you will have to go back to the equinox in the previous year. Once you have addressed the equinox question, the steps are as follows.

First, calculate the FR year: 

if (most recent equinox is in current year)
   year = current year - 1792 + 1;
else  // most recent equinox is in previous year 
   year = current year - 1792;

Next count the number of days since the most recent solstice, with the solstice itself counting as day one. Call this number D.

If D is more than 360, then subtract 360 from D to find the day in the end of year fesitval.

Otherwise, next calculate the month as follows: 

   month = (D - 1) / 30 + 1

Finally, calculate the day in the month as follows: 

   day = (D -  1) % 30 + 1

Example 1: For November 3, 2002, whose (Gregorian) day in the year is 307, the FR day in the year is 307-266+1=42, where 266 is the Gregorian day in the year of 9/23/02, the most recent autumnal equinox. The FR year is 2002-1792+1=211, the FR month is 42/30+1=2, and the FR day is 41%30+1=12. The day is then the 13th day of the 2th month of year 211.

Example 2: For February 3, 2003, whose (Gregorian) day in the year is 34, x=34+100=134, where 100=8+31+30+31 is the number of days from 9/23/2002 to 12/31/2002. The FR year then is 2003-1792=211, the FR month is 133/30+1=5, and the FR day is 133%30+1=14. The day then is the 14th day of the 5th month of year 211. Both of these calculations assume that modern leap year rules are used.
